Welcome!

My name is Marcus Roldan, and I am a 21-year-old, fifth-year Computer Science student at Northeastern University with a concentration in Artificial Intelligence.

Throughout my time at Northeastern I have had the pleasure of learning and experiencing so many aspects of technology, Computer Science, Software Development, Data Science, and many more interesting topics.

While on my first co-op as a Software Engineer at Wishroute, Inc., on top of gaining highly-valuable technical experience and knowledge, I realized the personal importance of having a tangible, positive impact through my work.

Around the same time, I began to become more fascinated with transportation systems, the built-environment, and the impact they have on our lives. As I followed this new-found passion, I joined the Transportation Engineering club at Northeastern to learn more about the subject through bi-weekly talks from people in the field. Sustainable transportation and urban development became one of my favorite areas, and I was extremely lucky to participate in a study-abroad program through Northeastern about this very subject.

Throughout the five weeks I spent in the Netherlands during this program, I learned about and expereinced (by bike, train, and bus, of course!) the infrastructure, design principles, and most importantly, the way of life that is fostered through a multi-modal, low-carbon, and sustainable policy implementations.

Ever since this study abroad, I have been striving to leverage my Computer Science skills and experience through various projects dealing with transportaion systems and active-transport infrastructure. Post graduation, I am keen to pursue a role which utilizes technology, Computer and Data Science, and Software Development to aid in the improvement of our transportation systems and built-environment.

Most recently, I have been working on a project alongside the Boston Cyclist Union's (BCU) volunteer data science team, BCU Labs. The project uses reports of Illegal Parking from Boston 311 to identify instances where active-transportation infrastructure is impacted, aiming to visualize the areas of the transportation system that need the most improvement.
